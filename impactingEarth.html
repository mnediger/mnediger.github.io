Impacting Earth

Data Visualization

This visualization aims to clarify the risk of future impacts with near earth objects, in the context of major impact events in Earthâ€™s past. Designed as a static two-page spread, it targets members of the public without any prior knowledge of planetary science.

Tools

Illustrator

Audience

Lay audience

Process

Following the Chelyabinsk meteor impact in Russia four years ago, the risk of future impacts to life on Earth has been a hot topic in the media. Some media outlets have used sensationalist tactics to capitalize on the risk of these impacts and draw in readers. The goal of this visualization was to clarify the risk of these impacts by providing context in the form of data about existing impact craters.

Data Sources

Data about the size, location, age, and composition of impact craters across the globe was sourced from the Earth Impact Database.

Data related to the possibility of future impacts was sourced from NASA's Center for Near Earth Object Studies (Sentry).

Design Rationale

The Earth Impact Database lists the name, location by country or region, longitude and latitude, diameter, age, exposure state, drilled state, rock type, and bolide type for each impact structure. Geological data was considered extraneous assuming a non-scientific audience, and location was deemed unnecessary based on the findings of an audit of existing visual media on the topic. The final design of this chart was driven by the goal of visualizing the frequencies of impact events of various sizes across earth's history. Designing for a lay audience, it was important to provide context (in the form of major events in the evolution of life on earth) for the scale of these events in time, and to represent time linearly.
