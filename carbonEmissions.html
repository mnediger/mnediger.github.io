Carbon Emissions

Data Visualization

This project challenged me to redesign an existing visualization of the world's carbon emissions from energy consumption. The data used in this project comes from the U.S. Energy Information Administration.

Tools

Excel - Illustrator

Client

Professor Jodie Jenkinson

Audience

Educated lay audience

Process

I started this redesign by analyzing the existing solution.

Strengths

  • The use of a visual metaphor invites exploration
  • The major trend is clear, as the very large circles stand out in each case
  • The use of colour to categorize by continent helps the viewer more directly compare between the two cases

Weaknesses

  • The use of area to represent emission levels can be misleading
  • The organization of the data makes direct comparisons difficult
  • The smallest contributors get lost due to size
  • Trends over time are ignored completely

The original piece successfully communicates the major contributors in each case, but limits any further analysis.

Goals for Redesign

I chose to take a different approach, focusing on trends over time and trying to enable comparisons whenever possible. I targeted an educated lay audience, hoping to make major trends accessible to the average viewer, but also to facilitate deeper explorations of the data. Overall, I tried to organize my charts by increasing complexity, granularity of data, and information coding, with a clear narrative throughout.
